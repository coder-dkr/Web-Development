Key features of the CBSE-i are as follows:-
  • Enquiry and skill based curriculum
  • Catering to individual learning styles
  • Learner and Learning centric with focus on analytical approach
  • Focus on experiential and real world learning
  • Focus on analysis and discovery approach
  • Five Learning areas (subjects) in a trans-disciplinary approach, incorporating Research Project, Life Skills, Physical Education and Social Empowerment through work and action (SEWA)
  • Facility for use of technology in teaching learning process, devoid of text books.
  • Qualified and trained teachers with hi-tech efficiency in the ratio of 1 teacher facilitator against 25 students per class.
  • Facility to download content through internet, worksheets or to visit CBSE-i portal.
  • Fee structure to commensurate with facilities provided.
Keeping in view, the global trends the school is introduced the CBSE-i curriculum for one batch of 25 students each in classes I, II and III for the session 2013-14. As per requirements for CBSE-i curriculum, the school followed a different fee structure and admission criteria.





The introduction of smart boards in the school was a significant shift to the IT world in DAV Pushpanjali. School found that it had made the learning process easier along with making the students visualise things more efficiently. It is a way making students learn new things by connecting them to the outer world with help of computer and Internet. Smart boards can be considered as the maximised or developed form of computers. In labs it has now become easy for teachers to demonstate practicals on smart boards and for the students to understand those practicals easily as well as quickly. It has also become much easier for counsellors to take workshops and sessions for related to topics like career, internet etc. with help of smart boards.





The school finds that the Robotics education is essential to inculcate a skill to design interesting, movable and animated electronics projects. It harbors creativity, encourages students to think, discuss among each other and build innovative designs to solve the problems. The Robotics activity starts from Class VII. The students begin by learning that a whole computer can reside on a chip on a robot. It is a pleasure to see the kids when they download and see in action their first program on the robot. Their sense of achievement and excitement is unmatched.
