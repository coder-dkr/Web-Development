3. Keeping in tradition of DAVPS Pushpanjali Enclaves quest for innovation, the school participated in an All India Competition on Innovative Practices and Experiments in Education for schools and Teacher education Institutions for the year 2014-15. The school picked up a remedial project DECODING MATHEMATICS where the students suffering from fear of Maths were selected and taught in a diverse manner. After effective execution and implementing our 35-point innovative teaching strategies, we were successful to explore students experiences of mathematics. On 26th June, 2015 we were invited to present our report at the National Seminar held at the NCERT Campus. Our project was competing with schools and teacher training institutes from all over India. It was a pleasure to have been selected for the NCERT Award for the year 2014-15. The award incorporates a cash award of Rs. 20,000 /- along with a certificate. After the success of the Maths Project ? DECODING MATHEMATICS? has now become a regular feature of the school and part of the curriculum so that the fear of Maths can be overcome. The school is also envisaging a desire to envision and implement a similar project in other subjects, too.

  Library  

The Library is the nerve centre of the school Our School has a well furnished Library, with a rich collection of various types of books, under the charge of a well-qualified Librarian. Library periods are provided to all the classes in which they visit the Library to read the magazines, books and encyclopedias etc. Besides, they utilise these periods for getting the books issued too, to study them at home. Following the wealth of wisdom in this saying, the school has collected a substantial no. of books on wide ranging disciplines to quench the children's quest for knowledge. A qualified dedicated librarian perseveres to upkeep the library along the principles of library science. Library period introduced in the daily routine will go a long way to inculcate reading habits, the real means of obtaining pleasure and knowledge.




   Initiatives to develop academics  

Special blazer & Scholarship for the Meritorious Students:- 
  • Any student securing A+ Grade in all the five main subjects of Class III & IV and 9.4 CGPA in Class V for 3 consecutive years is awarded a Blue Blazer in Class VI.
  • The students securing CGPA 9.2 in Class VI,VII,VIII for 3 consecutive years are awarded a maroon blazer in Class IX.
  • Similarly, students securing CGPA 9.2 in Class VII, VIII, IX for 3 consecutive years are awarded a maroon blazer in Class X and likewise for VIII, IX and X are awarded in Class XI.
  • Any student securing 9.2/9.6 CGPA for 6 consecutive years are awarded a green blazer.
  • Awardee of green Blazer again securing 85% in Class XI is awarded a Gold Medal in Class XII.
  • Any student who secures 85% and above in Class XI is awarded a Silver Medal.
  • There are various Awards for Subject Toppers of Class XII.

    Education gives direction and determines the future. Keeping in view the global requirements of learning we are evolving a learning process and an environment would empower the learners as future citizens to become global leaders, stress-free competent, confident promoting harmony and peace. Key features of the CBSE-i:- 
  • Enquiry and skill based curriculum
  • Catering to individual learning styles
  • Learner and Learning centric with focus on analytical approach
  • Focus on experiential and real world learning
  • Focus on analysis and discovery approach
  • Five Learning areas (subjects) in a trans-disciplinary approach, incorporating Research Project, Life Skills, Physical Education and Social Empowerment through work and action (SEWA)
  • Facility for use of technology in teaching learning process, devoid of text books.
  • Qualified and trained teachers with hi-tech efficiency in the ratio of 1 teacher facilitator against 25 students per class.
  • Facility to download content through internet, worksheets or to visit CBSE-i portal.
  • Fee structure to commensurate with facilities provided.

    Keeping in view, the global trends the school is introducing the CBSE-i curriculum for one batch of 25 students each in classes I, II and III for the session 2013-14. As per requirements for CBSE-i curriculum, the school will follow a different fee structure and admission criteria.
